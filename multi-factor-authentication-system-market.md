<p><p>Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) systems enhance security through various market types based on the number of factors involved. </p><p>- **Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)** typically combines something you know (like a password) with something you have (like a smartphone for a verification code).</p><p>- **Three-Factor Authentication (3FA)** adds a third element, often a biometric feature such as a fingerprint.</p><p>- **Four-Factor Authentication (4FA)** includes an additional layer, such as location-based verification.</p><p>- **Five-Factor Authentication (5FA)** incorporates multiple biometrics or behavioral analytics, offering heightened security for sensitive applications.</p></p>
