<p><ul><li>Photopolymerization</li><li>Light Decomposition Type</li><li>Photocrosslinked</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The G-line photoresist market comprises three main types: photopolymerization, light decomposition, and photocrosslinked. Photopolymerization involves the use of light to initiate a chemical reaction, creating a polymer structure that defines the pattern. Light decomposition photoresists rely on the breakdown of compounds when exposed to specific wavelengths, resulting in solubility changes. Photocrosslinked photoresists form a networked structure upon exposure, enhancing thermal stability and durability. Each type caters to varying applications in semiconductor manufacturing and microfabrication processes.</p></p>
